Poetry






Scars

They never leave your side,

They remind you of every single mistake,

They’re like ghosts who can’t find peace.

Scars are painful,

Scars are shameful.

I want to hide mines,

They make me feel weak.

Every time someone discovers them,

I can’t face them anymore.

I wish I could describe my scars like wings,

I wish they were the trophy of a survivor,

I wish I would wear them with pride

You once told me,

Turn your scars into stars.


Happiness

I once believed in happiness,

But today I must confess,

I’ve been ignorant of reality,

I’ve been ignorant of humanity.

I was drowned in darkness,

Compelled by sadness.

Humans are on the same boat,

They call it society.

I’ve dreamed of a knights oath

I’ve once dreamed of liberty

Isn’t it alluring?

As for now, I can’t get attention

What if I use poison

To show my depression

Happiness is a sweet dream,

An illusion.


Pain

The desire to leave this place,

The desire to live your own life

Is always tied with pain.

Despair is what makes people reflect

It makes them fall

Or it makes them stand on their own.

Sometimes they just put on a smile

And quitely die while smirking.

Today I'm embracing the angels,

I was craving for their lights long gone.

I saw demons behind people,

They were covering hope.

Not everyone could see them,

Those children were picky,

They liked to play with the less powerful.

I was a perfect target who lasted long

We played together forever.

I thought they were gonna leave

So I tried to please them.

Every day was like hell,

I didn't know my place back then.


Guilt

My voice keeps breaking,

While crying bloody tears,

I’m thirsty for love.

I tried to end my miserable life,

But the angels didn’t take my soul .

In the end I only hurt my pride.

People commit suicide to stop the pain.

I want to feel the pain,

Maybe it will stop the guilt.

When the fierce wind coldly whip the trees

When the owls hoot their lullabies

And when the angels, for you, arise

As you’re taken to a dreamy paradise


When the regal moon takes finally place

When your thoughts yield to drowsiness

When croon to ur ears the crickets

Forget your fears, welcome your death

Written by "Imane Saoudi "

Traveling through the fields

Little man had a question for his Mama while they were walking through the fields. The plants surrounding their way were twice the boy's height, and as he looked up to the mother, he saw the sunbeams illuminating her face, while he was shielded.

"Where are we going?'' he asked like any kid would on a trip.

She kept quiet. The sound of their feet on the ground was comforting, like a rhythm keeping track of their thoughts.

After a while staring back at the yellow light like she was battling, looking for an answer to her own questions behind a blinding reality, she finally got a glimpse of knowledge.

"We're looking for home, honey. We're looking for home."

She said it with less furrowed eyebrows, now watching the road.

''But isn't home where we were ? Aren't we supposed to be there ? With our family and our... everyday ?" The little man asked again.

A weak force was pulling back at the knot where their hands united. The boy's tilted head towards the sky was making him slow down, so she finally lowered her gaze to his sweet face and said with the cousin of a smile:

"We're always where we're supposed to be, baby. Might it be a road or a house, by the lake or by the sea. It's good. It's always good. Even if your feet hurt from walking."

The kid's face scrunched in a ball of wonder and distractedness, and they kept on walking.

The Cat Cloud story

- I see a cat, she said.

- It's because you want to see a cat. You wouldn't otherwise.

- What now! You're saying I only see what I want to see?

- Yes. Don't we all?

- If it was true then I would choose to not see the ugliness in this world.

- I don't think so.

- And why is that? Who wants to see hunger, death, poverty, cruelty, stupidity, savageness?

- Logically, no one wants to see all that. But what if it excused our own ugliness. You would accept the world's bad sides, so that you could accept yourself. You'd only see the bad in you otherwise. She kept quiet.

- You'd say ''It's the way it is'' and you'd be right, he continued.

***

- What do you see?

- Oh me?! I see a cat.


He had a bright smile. She wanted to punch him in the face.


Written by "Kamilia Bennis"

HUBRIS

No fragment upon which to float away from the wreck.

As if it was the Bermuda triangle, but I was a willing drowner.


Without any compelling proof,

I kept asking myself:

Was it the truth, excessive pride, or self-confidence

That got them either unsympathetic or way too sympathetic,

With no in between.


I was unable to tell them how nor why,

So I kept pretending to be myself again,

Imitating the person I’ve always been.

Written by "May"

Telle Terre telle mère

Je suis votre unique mère,

Et le centre de cet univers.

Considérée l’une des plus belles planètes,

Je demeure votre seule maison douillette.


Surnommée la planète bleue,

J’appartiens au système solaire.

Et étant une des créations de Dieu,

Je vous offre la meilleure atmosphère.


La galaxie où je me situe

S’appelle la voie Lactée.

Je suis une terre perdue

Dans un monde d’infinités.


Terre tellurique que je suis ,

Je vous offre , à vous , les êtres vivants, la vie.

Eau-sol - air - température et lumière,

Vous y trouverez toute la matière nécessaire.


Je suis entre vos mains ,

Vous êtes entre mes doigts.

Je vous ai acceptés avec entrain,

Alors ne détruisez pas votre seul toit.


Chers Humains,

C’est à vous que je m’adresse.

Ne soyez pas victime de votre maladresse ;

Ne soyez pas la cause de l’extinction de votre espèce ;

Gardez-moi pour les prochains !
